System Overview

Gas detection systems play a critical role in ensuring safety in various environments by monitoring the presence of hazardous gases. These systems are widely used in industrial plants, refineries, chemical manufacturing facilities, and urban areas to detect combustible, toxic, and oxygen-depleting gases.

Aim and Importance

The aim is to develop a real-time gas detection system for industrial applications and air pollution monitoring. The system addresses issues related to the cost, coverage, and accuracy of existing gas detection devices.

How the System Works

The system uses gas sensors to continuously monitor the presence of gases. These sensors detect gas concentrations by undergoing chemical or resistance changes when exposed to specific gases.

Sensor Technology

Electrochemical Sensors

These sensors detect gas concentration by measuring the current generated from an electrochemical reaction. They consist of three electrodes: working (sensing), counter, and reference electrodes, immersed in an electrolyte.

  • High specificity and accuracy
  • Cost-effective with low interference
  • Require temperature compensation

Metal Oxide Sensors

These sensors detect gases by measuring changes in electrical resistance. Oxygen molecules adsorb on the sensor surface and capture electrons, increasing resistance.

  • Durable and cost-effective
  • Commonly used for combustible gases
  • Affected by humidity and temperature

System Architecture

Hardware and Communication Setup

The system uses ESP32 microcontrollers as slave devices to interface with sensors, with a master ESP32 managing the display dashboard and overall coordination.

Applications

Industrial Use

Gas detection systems are crucial in various industrial sectors for ensuring safety and operational efficiency. They continuously monitor for toxic, flammable, and oxygen-related gases to prevent accidents.

Air Quality Monitoring

For air quality monitoring, gas detection systems assess pollutants such as carbon monoxide, nitrogen oxides, sulfur oxides, and particulate matter in urban and industrial areas.
